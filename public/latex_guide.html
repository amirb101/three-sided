üéØ What is LaTeX?

LaTeX is a typesetting system that makes it easy to write mathematical expressions, equations, and scientific notation. In Three-Sided, you can use LaTeX in your flashcard statements, hints, and proofs to create professional-looking mathematical content.

üí° Tip: LaTeX expressions are enclosed in dollar signs: $expression$ or \(expression\)

üìê Common Mathematical Expressions

Square Root

Square root:
$\sqrt{x}$ or $\sqrt[n]{x}$
Result: \(\sqrt{x}\) or \(\sqrt[n]{x}\)

Integrals

Definite integral:
$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) dx$
Result: \(\int_{a}^{b} f(x) dx\)
Indefinite integral:
$\int f(x) dx$
Result: \(\int f(x) dx\)

Summation

Sum:
$\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$
Result: \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i\)

Limits

Limit:
$\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$
Result: \(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)\)

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't forget the dollar signs! LaTeX expressions must be enclosed in $ or \( \)
‚ö†Ô∏è Use curly braces for grouping: $x^{2+3}$ gives \(x^{2+3}\), not \(x^2+3\)
‚ö†Ô∏è Spaces matter: $\frac{a}{b}$ works, but $\frac {a} {b}$ might not
